WATER

Floods

Floods are a common and devastating natural disaster that can be caused by various factors such as heavy rainfall or melting snow. They can result in extensive damage to infrastructure, homes, and loss of life. It is important to stay informed about flood risks and take necessary precautions to minimize their impact, including preparing emergency plans and practicing safety measures.

FIRE

Forest fires

Forest fires are a natural disaster that can cause immense damage to the environment and human life. They occur when dry fuel and high temperatures lead to uncontrollable blazes. Preventative measures, such as clearing brush and following fire safety guidelines, can reduce the risk of forest fires.

EARTH

Earthquakes

Earthquakes are a devastating natural disaster that can cause widespread destruction and loss of life. They occur when tectonic plates shift, causing vibrations and ground movement. Emergency plans, building reinforcement, and safety precautions are essential for earthquake preparedness.

Storms
